01 — PHILOSOPHY

What we are, and what we aren't

BBI is a predictive ranking, not a tip sheet. We don't pretend to know who will win. We rank every player in the field by expected value against the field and against the market, then show our work so you can disagree.

Three rules guide every design decision on this site:

Show the model, not the output
Every ranking cell, every DFS lineup, every bet slip stake exposes the underlying inputs. If we say Scottie is +3.2pp of edge on the moneyline, we'll tell you what probability we computed, what the book's implied probability is, and how many books we matched against.
Calibrate weekly against reality
The PROMETHEUS backtest scored BBI against 47 completed 2025 events. We track Spearman and Brier every week against the DataGolf baseline. If we don't beat it, we publish that too.
Keep shadow models running
Four competing weight configurations run in the shadow each week. Promotion requires ≥0.03 Spearman lift and ≥5% Brier improvement sustained over 3+ weeks and 8+ events. No vibes-based weight changes.
02 — THE 10 FACTORS

What BBI is measuring

Every player gets scored on 10 normalized factors (0–100). The BBI score is a weighted average — ceiling-heavy, value-aware, and tuned each week to the course DNA of the active event.

i
How normalization works. Each raw input (SG:Total, SG:APP trailing-12, course SG history, form score, etc.) is z-scored across the active field, then mapped to 0–100 via a logistic transform. This makes factor values comparable across fields of different strength — a 65 Talent at Augusta means the same as a 65 Talent at Sanderson Farms.

We pay for ceiling and salary value. GPP equity comes from tournament wins, not safe make-cuts — and in DFS, the DK salary divides everything. A 92 BBI player at $7,800 is almost always better than a 96 BBI at $11,200, because you need 5 more players to fit under the cap.

Course Fit dominates the weekly shift. The 48-venue Course DNA library tells us whether a course rewards wedges, long irons, or rough-penalty survival — and the weights adjust before the model produces rankings. This is why a short-iron specialist can rank top-10 one week and top-40 the next.

05 — MONTE CARLO

THEMIS: 10,000 tournaments per event

BBI gives us a ranking. THEMIS gives us a probability distribution. For every event we run 10,000 simulated tournaments — four rounds each — with round-by-round variance calibrated from 2015–2025 PGA Tour data.

# For each player i, each round r, each sim: scorei,r,s = μi + σi · εr,s + σc · ηs # σc · η = course-wide shock (cut-line variance) totali,s = Σr=1..4 scorei,r,s finishi,s = rank(total•,s) P(win)i = #{s : finishi,s = 1} / 10,000 P(top10)i = #{s : finishi,s ≤ 10} / 10,000 P(cut)i = #{s : totali,1..2 ≤ cuts} / 10,000

μi (mean strokes-gained per round) is anchored to DataGolf's skill predictions plus BBI's course-fit adjustment. σi is the player's own round variance from trailing 12 events. σc captures whole-field shocks — weather, green speeds, wind. The shock ηs is shared across players within a sim, which is why a windy Thursday moves everyone down together.

?
Why 10,000? Below ~2,000 sims, tail events (P(win) for 200:1 longshots) are too noisy to be useful. Above ~15,000, the marginal information gain is near-zero. 10k is the cost/value sweet spot on a 20-minute compute budget.
06 — DEVIGGING & KELLY

OMNIA: finding edges against 15 books

Raw sportsbook odds carry a 6–15% vig (overround). Before we can compare BBI probabilities to market prices, we have to strip the vig. Then we size stakes via fractional Kelly — never full Kelly.

Step 1 — Devig each book

Every price from every book is converted to implied probability, then normalized so the book's market sums to 1.0. This is the multiplicative method — simple, and for sparse golf markets (where most players don't have prices), it's more stable than Shin or power methods.

praw,i = 1 / decimal_oddsi Σ = Σj ∈ book praw,j # overround (usually 1.06–1.15) pfair,i = praw,i / Σ # removes the vig

Step 2 — Consensus across books

For each player, OMNIA collects devigged probabilities from up to 15 sportsbooks (DraftKings, FanDuel, Pinnacle, BetMGM, Caesars, etc.) and takes the median to form a market consensus. Pinnacle is shown separately as a sharper reference.

Step 3 — BBI edge

edge_pp = (p_BBI − p_market) × 100 # positive pp = BBI thinks this is more likely than the market # negative pp = market thinks it's more likely than BBI

Step 4 — Kelly sizing

Full Kelly maximizes long-term log-growth of bankroll but is brutal on variance — a 20% Kelly bet can swing ±40% of your bankroll intra-month. OMNIA defaults to ½ Kelly and caps at ¼ Kelly on public-market plays.

b = decimal_odds − 1 # net odds kelly_pct = (p · b − (1 − p)) / b applied_pct = kelly_pct · fraction # fraction ∈ {¼, ½, 1} stake = bankroll · applied_pct EV = stake · (p_BBI · dec − 1)
!
Negative Kelly is auto-zero. If kelly_pct comes back negative, the bet has negative expected value — OMNIA drops it from the slip rather than let you "bet the other side" (golf markets are too noisy for arbitrage).
07 — LIVE UPDATES

PERSEPHONE: re-scoring mid-round

Once Thursday tees off, a pre-tournament model is frozen. PERSEPHONE takes the baton — polling live endpoints every 30 minutes, recomputing win / top-10 / make-cut probabilities, and surfacing leverage shifts.

Ingest live round state
Four DataGolf endpoints feed PERSEPHONE: live stats, live score, live predictions, and hole-by-hole scoring. Polled every 30 minutes from Thursday 2pm through Sunday 7pm ET.
Recompute with remaining variance
THEMIS re-runs with the unfinished holes as the only source of variance. A leader through 63 holes has near-zero remaining σ; a cut-line bubble player still has two rounds of noise.
Probability shifts & health pills
Every lineup card gets a health indicator: green (on pace) / amber (cut bubble) / red (MDF risk or missed). Top-10 movers are ranked by live − pre probability delta.
Leverage watch
PERSEPHONE flags which players are most-owned and underperforming (fade signal) and which are low-owned and surging (late-window leverage). Top-10 surgers and faders feed the Live page's Leverage Watch panel.

Why manual promotion? Because a variant that over-fits to recent conditions — say, six straight windy coastal events — can look statistically dominant and then collapse on the first calm week. A human looks at why the variant is winning before it replaces the baseline.

09 — ACCURACY SCORING

Spearman, Brier, and calibration

Three metrics decide whether BBI is actually predictive or just confident-looking. We publish all three on the Performance page — no cherry-picking.

Spearman rank correlation

For each completed event, we correlate BBI's predicted finishing order with the actual finishing order. Perfect = 1.0. Random = 0.0. DataGolf's own published prediction baseline on 2025 events was ρ = 0.393 — that's our benchmark.

Brier score

Brier measures how well our probability forecasts match reality. Think of it as a penalty for being wrong, weighted by how confident you were.

Concrete example: We predict a player has a 10% chance to win the tournament. If that player actually wins, we're penalized: (1 − 0.10)² = 0.81. If they don't win, the penalty is smaller: (0 − 0.10)² = 0.01. We compute this penalty for every player in every event, then average across the whole field. Lower Brier = better predictions. A perfect forecast is 0.0. A random guess is around 0.25.

We track Brier for five markets: win, top-5, top-10, top-20, make-cut. Our 2025 backtest baselines (vs. DataGolf): win 0.013, top-10 0.088, top-20 0.143, make-cut 0.172. BBI must match or beat these benchmarks each week.

Brier = (1/N) · Σ (pi − oi)² # p = predicted prob, o = outcome (1 if event happened else 0) # lower Brier = better calibration

Calibration (reliability)

A calibrated 20% probability should hit 20% of the time. The Performance page bucket plots BBI's predicted probabilities into 10 bins and compares each bin's predicted mean to the observed frequency. Reliability = mean absolute deviation across bins. A well-calibrated model runs <0.03 reliability.

10 — LIMITS

Where BBI is weak

No model is the boss of everything. These are the failure modes we've catalogued, and how the site warns you.

Sparse course history
Course-fit factor breaks down for rookies and venues with fewer than 3 career rounds by the field. The Rankings page shows a dim gold course-fit cell when sample size is low — treat that column as "unknown" for that player, not "bad".
Weather surprises
THEMIS bakes historical σ for weather shock but doesn't re-simulate when Thursday's forecast shifts to 25mph gusts overnight. PERSEPHONE corrects live — but your pre-tournament picks were made blind to that.
Market is sometimes right
A +4pp edge vs. the market often means the market knows something — injury, WD rumor, swing coach change. OMNIA flags positions with >5pp edge and <5 matching books as "thin consensus" — that's a warning, not an endorsement.
Designated events warp field strength
Signature events have top-heavy fields with no cuts, so make-cut factor is undefined and cut-safety weight collapses to zero. BBI re-weights on the fly but historical comparisons (signature vs. regular event) should be treated separately.
