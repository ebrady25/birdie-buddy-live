01 · Devigging

Why raw odds lie.

A book publishing 10/1 is not claiming a 9.09% probability — that price includes the vigorish (house margin). We sum the implied probability of every player in the field, then scale each back so the total equals 100%. That's the true price the book is offering.

02 · Pinnacle anchor

Why the sharps matter.

Pinnacle runs razor-thin margins and welcomes professional action, which means their line is the closest thing to an efficient market price. A gap between Pinnacle and DraftKings often signals an exploitable mispricing at the recreational book.

03 · Kelly sizing

Bet the edge, not your gut.

Kelly tells you the stake size that maximises long-run bankroll growth given a fixed edge. We publish fractional Kelly (½ default) — the full value is mathematically optimal but practically brutal, so half is the industry standard hedge against model error.

How to read this page. Green cells are where BBI thinks the book is too long; red cells are where the book is too tight. The Edge column is raw (BBI %) minus (market consensus %), so a +5.00pp edge means BBI says the player wins 5 points more often than the 15-book consensus implies. None of this is financial advice — models can be wrong, markets are smart, and variance is real. Never stake more than you can cheerfully lose.
