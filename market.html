01 · Devigging

Why raw odds lie.

A book publishing 10/1 is not claiming a 9.09% probability — that price includes the vigorish (house margin). We sum the implied probability of every player in the field, then scale each back so the total equals 100%. That's the true price the book is offering.

02 · Pinnacle anchor

Why the sharps matter.

Pinnacle runs razor-thin margins and welcomes professional action, which means their line is the closest thing to an efficient market price. A gap between Pinnacle and DraftKings often signals an exploitable mispricing at the recreational book.

03 · Parlay shopping

One book to rule them all.

For parlays, decimal odds multiply — so small differences per leg compound dramatically. Add all your legs, and the slip will automatically find which book gives you the highest combined payout across the full ticket.

How to read this page. Green cells are where BBI thinks the book is too long; red cells are where the book is too tight.
